🔴 High (3)

🔴 High #1 Components Center of page — primary logo / hero area
DesignA large animated Google Doodle occupying roughly 460x180px, featuring stylized 3D letters (G-O-O-G-L-E) in vivid multicolor with tropical/festive decorations including a pink triangle, palm trees, a heart, and a musical note icon
LiveThe standard flat Google wordmark logo (blue G, red o, yellow o, blue g, green l, red e) rendered at approximately 272x92px — significantly smaller and entirely different in visual style
FixIf this Doodle is the intended design state, the live page must load the correct Doodle asset. If the Doodle has since rotated out, the design file should be updated to reflect the current static wordmark to keep the reference in sync with production.
🔴 High #2 Layout Center of page — vertical positioning of the logo/Doodle
DesignThe Doodle sits noticeably higher on the page, with its vertical center at roughly y=240px (out of 900px), leaving a large open region between the Doodle bottom and the search bar
LiveThe standard wordmark sits lower, with its center at approximately y=285px, and the overall vertical composition is more compact between the logo and the search bar
FixAlign the logo vertical center to match the design specification at ~y=240px, or confirm that the positioning change is intentional with the new wordmark.
🔴 High #3 Components Right side of Doodle area — share icon (~x=955, y=313)
DesignA circular share/network icon button appears to the right of the Doodle
LiveNo share icon is present on the live page; the area is empty
FixRestore the share icon if it is a required UI element, or remove it from the design file if it was Doodle-specific and no longer applicable.

🟡 Medium (2)

🟡 Medium #4 Components Below the search buttons — promotional banner area (~y=530)
DesignNo promotional content below the 'I'm Feeling Lucky' button; the area is clean white space
LiveA Google Fitbit Air promotional banner is displayed: a Fitbit heart-rate icon followed by 'New! Google Fitbit Air offers a deeper look at your health in a lighter, smarter way' in red/blue hyperlink text
FixDetermine whether this promotional slot is a permanent UI element that should be reflected in the design, or a time-limited banner that can be excluded from the design spec.
🟡 Medium #5 Color Top navigation bar — Google Apps grid icon (waffle menu)
DesignThe 3x3 grid waffle icon appears with a darker, more saturated fill consistent with the nav link color (~#5f6368)
LiveThe waffle icon renders slightly lighter/thinner in stroke weight compared to the design, creating a subtle contrast difference against the adjacent nav links
FixVerify the waffle icon SVG fill color matches the nav text color token; adjust to #5f6368 if it deviates.

🟢 Low (1)

🟢 Low #6 Content Page footer — center footer text
DesignFooter center uses a leaf emoji followed by 'Applying AI towards science and the environment'
LiveLive page matches the copy exactly; however, emoji rendering (the leaf) varies across operating systems and browser versions, potentially causing inconsistent visual appearance
FixConsider replacing the leaf emoji with an inline SVG icon to guarantee consistent cross-platform rendering, especially for brand-sensitive footer messaging.
